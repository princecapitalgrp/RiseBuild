# Sales Motion — Rise by Solis

---

## Current Stage: Pre-Revenue, Pre-Launch

The formal sales motion does not execute yet. What executes
now is trust infrastructure — the content, positioning, and
community presence that makes the eventual sales motion
credible when there is data to support it.

---

## Phase 0: Trust Infrastructure (Now → TestFlight Launch)

**What you do:** Founder content. Not marketing. Thinking out loud
about the problem you are solving in public spaces where your
target user already gathers.

### Three specific pieces before TestFlight opens:

**1. Substack essay — the core commercial insight:**
"Most people don't have a motivation problem. They have a
decision-load problem." 600–900 words. No product pitch.
Pure insight. Ends with: "I'm building something for this.
Early access available."

**2. Short-form video (60–90 seconds):**
Show what a genuinely personalized morning looks like versus
a generic one. No screencasts. No UI demo. The concept,
made visual.

**3. Privacy architecture in plain language:**
"Your system should work for you, not on you. Here is
exactly what Solis stores, where it lives, and why
it never leaves your device without your permission."

**Channels:**
- Substack (long-form trust)
- TikTok or Instagram Reels (reach)
- Reddit participation in r/getdisciplined, r/selfimprovement,
  r/Entrepreneur (community trust)

**Goal:** 200–500 people who care about what you're building
before TestFlight opens. These people convert to paying users
at 10–20× the rate of cold traffic.

## Phase 1: B2C Sales Motion (TestFlight → First 500 Users)

**Trigger:** Cohort B retention above 20% at day-7.

**What changes:** You begin talking about the product publicly
with data behind you. Not "I built a morning app." "Here is
what happened when 50 people used it for 30 days."

The B2C sales motion at this stage is entirely founder-led
and community-driven. No paid acquisition. No influencer
deals. Every dollar spent on acquisition before retention
is proven is wasted.

### Pricing

| Tier | Price | Notes |
|------|-------|-------|
| Founding member | $79/year | First 500 users, price-locked permanently |
| Standard annual | $99/year | After founding cohort fills |
| Monthly | Not offered at launch | Annual subscribers churn 51% less, are 2.4× more profitable |

Always lead with annual. Never lead with monthly.

### Paywall Placement

Day 14. After the user has experienced two weeks of morning
protocols and the personalization has begun to feel specific
to them. The paywall should feel like "save your results"
not "pay to continue."

### Conversion Target

12%+ from 14-day trial to annual subscription.

Below 10% means the product is not yet delivering enough
felt value before the wall. Do not increase marketing — fix
the product.

---

## Phase 2: Formal B2C Sales Motion (Post-PMF)

**Trigger:** Sean Ellis >40% "very disappointed," day-30
retention above 30%, operating mode distribution shift
demonstrable.

**What changes:** The sales motion becomes replicable. You
begin testing paid acquisition channels with a clear LTV
to support it.

At $99/year with 25% annual churn, LTV ≈ $297.
Supports up to $40–50 CAC.

### Channel Sequence (test in this order)

1. **Referral mechanic** — existing users refer peers.
   Highest conversion, lowest CAC.
2. **Creator partnerships** — not influencers. People with
   genuine authority in self-improvement and Gen Z
   productivity spaces.
3. **Substack/newsletter sponsorships** — high-trust,
   right demographic.
4. **Paid social** — only after channels 1–3 are proven.

## Phase 3: B2B Sales Motion (12+ Months Post-Launch)

**Trigger:** 2,000+ active users, retention data, operating
mode shift demonstrable at cohort level.

This motion does not start until there are numbers. Without
numbers, the B2B conversation is a product pitch. With
numbers, it is a business case.

### The Reframe (Challenger Opening)

> "Your people don't have a discipline problem. They have a
> decision-load problem that starts before work begins. Solis
> addresses it at the highest-leverage moment — the morning —
> and produces measurable consistency improvements at a cost
> that is a fraction of what disengagement and fragmented
> attention actually costs you."

### The B2B Differentiator: Beyond Privacy

**ROI Stack 1 — Labor recovery value:**
Knowledge workers lose an estimated 2.1 hours per day to
decision fatigue and attention fragmentation (Basex Research).
Even a 10% recovery at average knowledge worker cost produces
labor value that dwarfs the subscription cost at any reasonable
team size.

**ROI Stack 2 — Privacy as liability reduction:**
Every competitor in the B2B wellness space stores employee
health and emotional data in the cloud. That is a GDPR
liability, an HR liability, and a trust liability. Solis's
local-first architecture means the employer has no access to
individual employee data by design — which is not just ethical,
it is a procurement objection handler.

**ROI Stack 3 — Measurable retention signal:**
Employers who offer Solis as a benefit can see aggregate
(never individual) operating mode distribution across their
team. That is measurable wellness ROI, not a survey.

### The CFO Line

> "We are not asking you to fund mindfulness. We are asking you
> to fund more consistent cognitive readiness at a fraction of
> the cost of attention fragmentation, burnout, and failed
> wellness programs that nobody uses."

### Target B2B ICPs (in sequence)

| ICP | Size | Why First |
|-----|------|-----------|
| Boutique finance firms | 10–50 people | No HR bureaucracy, founder-led decision |
| Early-stage startups with distributed teams | 20–100 people | Burnout is visible and costly |
| Management consulting firms | Any | Cognitive readiness is explicit job requirement |

### B2B Pricing

- **Standard:** $12/user/month, minimum 20 users.
- **Pilot:** $2,000 flat for 90 days, no commitment.

Lead with the pilot. It removes procurement friction and
produces the case study you need for the next deal.

## Sales Motion by User Milestone

| Users | Motion |
|-------|--------|
| 0–50 | Founder sells everything personally. Every conversation is product research. |
| 50–500 | Founder content drives inbound. Referral mechanic activates. No outbound. |
| 500–2,000 | Creator partnerships and newsletter sponsorships tested. B2B conversations begin with existing network. |
| 2,000–10,000 | B2B motion formalised. Paid acquisition tested on proven channels. Investor narrative constructed from real data. |
| 10,000+ | Series A story. Acquisition conversation with Apple Health or a strategic buyer becomes credible. |

---

## What This Sales Motion Is Not

- It is not a growth hack. Viral mechanics at pre-PMF kill
  the product by flooding it with users before the
  personalization is good enough.
- It is not an influencer play. Influencer-driven installs
  produce low-intent users who churn before they experience
  the personalization depth.
- It is not a corporate wellness play yet. The B2B motion
  without retention data is a feature pitch, not a
  business case.

The constraint that feels like a limitation — 90 days of
runway, no paid acquisition, founder-led everything — is
actually the correct motion for this stage. You cannot
outspend your way to PMF. You can only earn it.
